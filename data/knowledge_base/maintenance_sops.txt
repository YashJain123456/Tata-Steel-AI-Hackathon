SOP-RM-001: ROLLING MILL — WORK ROLL CHANGE PROCEDURE
Equipment: Hot Strip Mill Finishing Stands F1–F6
Frequency: As required (typically every 60–90 km rolled)
PTW Required: YES | LOTO Required: YES
Time Required: 45–90 minutes per stand
================================================================================

PREREQUISITES:
- Permit to Work (PTW) approved by Shift Manager
- LOTO applied on stand drive, hydraulic system, cooling water
- Roll change crane inspected and certified
- Replacement rolls pre-heated to 40–60°C
- Chock inspection completed on replacement rolls

STEP 1: PREPARATION (15 min)
  1.1 Notify Production to reduce speed and cobble the strip tail
  1.2 Apply LOTO tag on all 6 energy isolation points (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic)
  1.3 Release hydraulic roll force to zero
  1.4 Lower roll gap to maximum open position
  1.5 Connect roll change car to stand (auto-coupling)

STEP 2: ROLL EXTRACTION (20 min)
  2.1 Engage roll change car coupling to work roll chock
  2.2 Retract work roll assembly (operator cabin push-button)
  2.3 Transfer worn roll assembly to roll storage via overhead crane
  2.4 Inspect housing liners for wear (replace if groove depth >2mm)
  2.5 Clean housing window with compressed air and wipe

STEP 3: ROLL INSERTION (20 min)
  3.1 Collect pre-inspected replacement roll assembly from storage
  3.2 Verify chock serial number against roll schedule
  3.3 Apply fresh lubrication to chock sliding surfaces (Molykote paste)
  3.4 Insert roll assembly via roll change car (controlled speed <5 mm/s)
  3.5 Lock chock clamps (torque: 800 Nm)
  3.6 Re-engage hydraulic connections (verify no leak — zero pressure first)

STEP 4: COMMISSIONING (15 min)
  4.1 Remove LOTO (authorised person only)
  4.2 Raise hydraulic pressure to 50% working pressure — check for leaks
  4.3 Raise to 100% working pressure
  4.4 Set roll gap to pass schedule (auto-positioning)
  4.5 Run stand at 50% speed for 2 minutes — verify bearing temperatures
  4.6 Increase to full production speed
  4.7 Monitor first 3 strips for flatness and surface quality

SIGN-OFF: Maintenance Engineer signature required. Log entry in CMMS.

SOP-RM-002: ROLLING MILL — GEAR BOX OIL SAMPLING PROCEDURE
Frequency: Monthly
PTW Required: NO | LOTO Required: NO (sampling port only)
================================================================================

STEP 1: Allow gear box to reach operating temperature (oil temp >40°C)
STEP 2: Flush sampling port with 100 mL of oil before taking sample
STEP 3: Collect 500 mL in clean, labelled sample bottle
STEP 4: Record: equipment ID, gear box ID, sample date, oil hours run
STEP 5: Submit to laboratory within 4 hours for:
  - Viscosity at 40°C and 100°C
  - Water content (Karl Fischer, target <200 ppm)
  - Particle count (NAS class, target NAS 9 or better)
  - Wear metals: Fe, Cu, Cr, Pb, Al, Sn
  - Additive metals: Zn, P, Ca, Mg
STEP 6: Report results to Maintenance Planner; schedule oil change if Fe >50 ppm
        or water >500 ppm.

SOP-BF-001: BLAST FURNACE — TUYERE CHANGE PROCEDURE (HOT)
Equipment: Blast Furnace Tuyere (any of 28 positions)
PTW Required: YES | LOTO Required: PARTIAL (blast valve closed)
Time Required: 45 minutes per tuyere
Minimum crew: 4 persons (1 supervisor, 2 fitters, 1 safety observer)
================================================================================

WARNING: This is a hot work procedure. Iron temperature >1500°C. PPE mandatory:
- Full face shield with shade 5 filter
- Aluminised suit
- Leather gloves + kevlar gloves
- Safety boots (steel toe, heat resistant)
- Personal CO detector (alarm at 25 ppm)

STEP 1: ISOLATION
  1.1 Request production to reduce blast to minimum (200 Nm³/min)
  1.2 Close individual tuyere blast valve (verified closed by supervisory panel)
  1.3 Open cooling water vent on blowpipe
  1.4 Verify no blast flow with portable anemometer

STEP 2: TUYERE REMOVAL
  2.1 Disconnect blowpipe from tuyere (blowpipe support in place)
  2.2 Using tuyere extraction tool, apply heat to joint (propane torch)
  2.3 Apply 5-ton hydraulic jack to extract tuyere
  2.4 Immediately quench extracted tuyere in water bath (extreme heat caution)

STEP 3: INSPECTION & REPLACEMENT
  3.1 Inspect tuyere seat — clean with copper brush
  3.2 Check cooling water ports in nose — flush with water (no obstruction)
  3.3 Insert new tuyere with fresh graphite rope seal
  3.4 Torque to 2500 Nm with calibrated torque wrench

STEP 4: REINSTATEMENT
  4.1 Reconnect blowpipe — check alignment (tolerance ±5mm)
  4.2 Open tuyere blast valve slowly (30 seconds full open)
  4.3 Verify blast flow restored
  4.4 Monitor cooling water outlet temperature for 10 minutes (should stabilise <35°C)

SOP-HYD-001: HYDRAULIC OIL CHANGE PROCEDURE
Equipment: Central Hydraulic Power Unit
Frequency: Every 4000 hours or oil analysis trigger
PTW Required: YES
Time Required: 4 hours
================================================================================

STEP 1: Depressurise system (dump accumulators via dump valve — verify 0 bar)
STEP 2: Connect oil drain hose to reservoir drain port
STEP 3: Drain full reservoir volume (approx. 5000 L) into oil drum for disposal
STEP 4: Flush reservoir with 500 L clean flushing oil; agitate and drain
STEP 5: Replace ALL filter elements (suction, return, high-pressure)
STEP 6: Replace breather filter on reservoir
STEP 7: Inspect reservoir interior with torch — remove any sludge
STEP 8: Fill with new HLP 46 oil (verify ISO grade and cleanliness certificate NAS 6)
STEP 9: Prime pump (open suction isolation fully before start)
STEP 10: Start pump at low pressure — cycle all cylinders 3 times
STEP 11: Take oil sample and submit for cleanliness verification
STEP 12: Update CMMS oil change record with volume, brand, batch number

SOP-LF-001: LADLE FURNACE — ELECTRODE CHANGE
Equipment: Ladle Furnace, Electrode mast (A, B or C phase)
PTW Required: YES | LOTO Required: YES (HV transformer isolated)
Time Required: 30 minutes
================================================================================

SAFETY: HV transformer must be de-energised and earthed before any electrode work.
Electrode can be at >600°C after heat — allow cool-down or use asbestos gloves.

STEP 1: Raise electrode to maximum position
STEP 2: Apply LOTO on HV transformer MCC
STEP 3: Apply electrode arm locking pin (prevents gravitational drop)
STEP 4: Unscrew stub nipple connection (anti-clockwise, threaded joint)
STEP 5: Lower worn electrode stub to safe position and remove with crane
STEP 6: Thread new electrode onto stub — torque 800 Nm (anti-seize compound)
STEP 7: Inspect contact pad on electrode holder — replace if worn >2 mm
STEP 8: Remove locking pin, lower electrode to working position
STEP 9: Remove LOTO, energise transformer
STEP 10: Verify arc striking on first heat — check current balance all 3 phases

SOP-CCM-001: CONTINUOUS CASTER — EMERGENCY BREAKOUT RESPONSE
Time Critical: Immediate response required
PTW Required: NO (emergency procedure)
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (first 30 seconds):
  1. Press EMERGENCY STOP on caster control pulpit
  2. Alert shift manager via radio: "Breakout at CCM, strand [X]"
  3. Do NOT approach the breakout zone — molten steel hazard
  4. Close tundish slide gate (manual override if auto fails)
  5. Activate emergency water spray on breakout zone

WITHIN 5 MINUTES:
  6. Confirm all personnel evacuated from casting floor
  7. Activate CO2 / nitrogen purge on secondary cooling zone
  8. Assign two experienced operators to observe — report to shift manager

WITHIN 30 MINUTES:
  9. Allow steel to solidify (minimum 20 minutes before approach)
  10. Incident commander to lead inspection team (PPE: aluminised suit)
  11. Document: strand number, casting speed at time, tundish temp, mold level trend
  12. Prepare breakout investigation report within 4 hours

POST-EVENT:
  - Mold copper inspection and replacement if scored
  - Segment roll inspection in affected zone
  - Root cause analysis before restart
  - Breakout prediction system recalibration

SOP-COMP-001: COMPRESSOR — STARTUP PROCEDURE (AFTER MAINTENANCE)
================================================================================

PRE-START CHECKS:
  □ All maintenance tags removed
  □ Oil level correct (sight glass 60–80%)
  □ Inlet air filter clean (ΔP <30 mbar)
  □ Anti-surge valve functional (manual test)
  □ Cooling water flow confirmed (inlet temp <30°C)
  □ Safety relief valve tested (set at 10.5 bar)

STARTUP SEQUENCE:
  1. Start cooling water pump — verify flow >200 L/min
  2. Start oil pump — verify oil pressure >2.5 bar
  3. Start motor at no-load (inlet guide vanes fully closed)
  4. After 30 seconds: open inlet guide vanes to 30%
  5. Monitor vibration (must be <3 mm/s at startup)
  6. Gradually open to demand setpoint over 2 minutes
  7. Verify discharge pressure reaches setpoint (typically 8 bar)
  8. Log startup time and initial readings in logbook

SHUTDOWN SEQUENCE:
  1. Close inlet guide vanes to minimum
  2. Open anti-surge valve
  3. Stop motor
  4. After 5 minutes: stop oil pump (allow bearing cool-down)
  5. Close cooling water after oil temp <40°C
