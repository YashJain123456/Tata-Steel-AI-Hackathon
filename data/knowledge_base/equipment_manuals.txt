SECTION 1: ROLLING MILL (SMS Group 6-Stand Tandem Hot Strip Mill)
Capacity: 5 MTPA | Strip Width: 600–1650 mm | Exit Thickness: 1.2–25 mm
================================================================================

1.1 EQUIPMENT OVERVIEW
The Rolling Mill comprises six finishing stands arranged in tandem. Each stand
uses a 4-high configuration with work rolls and back-up rolls. Drive is via
individual AC motors rated 8 MW per stand.

1.2 CRITICAL SENSOR PARAMETERS & ALARM LIMITS
  - Bearing Temperature (°C): Normal 60–120, Warning 121–140, Alarm >140, Trip >150
  - Mill Vibration (mm/s RMS): Normal 0–5, Warning 5.1–7, Alarm 7.1–8, Trip >8
  - Roll Force Hydraulic Pressure (bar): Normal 100–220, Alarm >250, Low alarm <100
  - Main Drive Motor Current (A): Normal 200–450, Warning >450, Trip >500
  - Roll Speed (RPM): Normal 800–1400, Max 1500, Low 800
  - Roll Gap (mm): ±0.02 mm tolerance
  - Strip tension (kN/m): 10–80 per stand

1.3 COMMON FAILURE MODES
  a) Work Roll Spalling: Surface cracks from thermal fatigue, cooling water contamination,
     or overload. Symptoms: vibration spike >6 mm/s, surface marking on strip.
     Action: Emergency roll change within 2 hours.
  b) Bearing Failure: Progressive deterioration. Early warning: temperature rise >5°C/hour,
     vibration change, oil analysis showing metallic particles.
     MTBF: 4000–6000 hours depending on load.
  c) Chock Seal Failure: Oil leakage around roll chocks, contamination of cooling water.
     Inspect seals every 6 months.
  d) Gear Coupling Misalignment: Vibration at 1× and 2× RPM, gear tooth wear.
     Alignment check required after every roll change.
  e) Loopers Hydraulic Failure: Strip tension loss, cobble risk. Inspect accumulator
     pre-charge pressure (should be 90 bar) every PM.

1.4 INSPECTION SCHEDULE
  - Daily: Cooling water flow, bearing temperature, lubrication levels
  - Weekly: Vibration signature analysis, roll force calibration check
  - Monthly: Gear box oil sample, coupling alignment check
  - Per Roll Change: Chock inspection, seal replacement if worn, bearing clearance check
  - Annual: Full mill alignment survey, gear box overhaul assessment

1.5 LUBRICATION REQUIREMENTS
  - Roll neck bearings: ISO VG 320 gear oil, automatic lubrication, 15 L/hour per bearing
  - Gear boxes: ISO VG 220 synthetic oil, 2000 L total, change every 8000 hours
  - Hydraulic system: HLP 46 hydraulic oil, filter change every 2000 hours

SECTION 2: BLAST FURNACE (Working Volume 3200 m³, Annual Output 2.8 MT)
================================================================================

2.1 EQUIPMENT OVERVIEW
The Blast Furnace produces pig iron by reducing iron ore with coke using hot
blast air. Key systems: burden charging, hot blast system, gas cleaning, cast house.

2.2 CRITICAL SENSOR PARAMETERS & ALARM LIMITS
  - Hot Blast Temperature (°C): Normal 900–1250, Warning >1250, Alarm >1300
  - Top Pressure (bar): Normal 1.5–4.0, Alarm >4.5, Low <1.5
  - Burden Descent Rate (mm/min): Normal 40–110, Slow <40 (slip risk), Fast >120
  - Oxygen Enrichment (%): Normal 2–7, Alarm >8
  - Tuyere Temperature (°C): Normal 250–350, Warning >350
  - Shaft Cooling Water ΔT: Normal <8°C, Alarm >12°C (cooler failure risk)
  - Top Gas Utilisation (%CO2): Target 47–52%

2.3 COMMON FAILURE MODES
  a) Tuyere Burnout: Catastrophic failure. Causes: back-pressure surge, water leak in
     blowpipe. Indicators: sudden blast pressure drop, tuyere temperature spike.
     Action: Emergency tuyere change (2–4 hours, hot repair team required).
  b) Cooler Panel Failure: Water leakage into furnace, hydrogen explosion risk.
     Monitor cooling water pH (target 7.5–8.5) and flow rate. ΔT >15°C = immediate action.
  c) Hanging / Slipping: Burden sticks then suddenly descends. Causes: low permeability,
     moisture spike, incorrect burden distribution.
     Response: Reduce blast volume 10%, increase oxygen, check burden profile.
  d) Scaffold Formation: Irregular accretions on furnace walls. Symptoms: asymmetric gas
     distribution, erratic burden descent.
  e) Tap Hole Problems: Short cast time (<30 min), thin iron stream indicates tap hole erosion.
     Clay gun maintenance critical — inspect daily.

2.4 SAFETY NOTES
  - CO gas present throughout operation — personal CO detector mandatory
  - Hot metal temperature typically 1480–1520°C
  - PTW required for all work within 10m of tap hole during casting
  - Tuyere platform: hearing protection mandatory (>95 dB)

SECTION 3: CONTINUOUS CASTING MACHINE (CCM — 4-strand slab caster)
Slab size: 200–250 mm × 900–1650 mm | Speed: 0.8–2.5 m/min
================================================================================

3.1 EQUIPMENT OVERVIEW
The CCM converts liquid steel from the ladle via tundish into solidified slabs.
Key systems: tundish, mold, secondary cooling, strand guide, cut-off.

3.2 CRITICAL SENSOR PARAMETERS & ALARM LIMITS
  - Tundish Temperature (°C): Normal 1480–1550, Warning >1550, Alarm >1560 (overflow risk)
  - Casting Speed (m/min): Normal 0.8–2.3, Max 2.5, Emergency stop if oscillation fault
  - Mold Cooling Water Flow (L/min): Normal 1200–1900, Low Alarm <1200, High Alarm >2000
  - Mold Level (mm from top): Target 100±10 mm, Alarm ±20 mm
  - Mold Vibration (mm/s): Normal 0–4, Alarm >5
  - Strand Guide Roll Gap: ±0.5 mm tolerance from designed taper
  - Secondary Cooling Water: Zone-by-zone control ±5% of setpoint

3.3 COMMON FAILURE MODES
  a) Breakout: Solidified shell rupture causing molten steel spillage.
     Causes: mold level fluctuation, cold steel, incorrect taper.
     Prevention: Breakout prediction system monitoring, mold thermocouple array.
  b) SEN Clogging: Submerged entry nozzle blocked by alumina build-up.
     Symptoms: asymmetric mold level, increased casting speed variation.
     Action: Argon injection increase, SEN change if not resolved within 5 min.
  c) Mold Copper Wear: Reduced heat extraction, taper mismatch.
     Inspect mold copper plates every 150–200 heats; replace at 3mm wear.
  d) Segment Roll Bearing Failure: Loss of strand guidance.
     Vibration monitoring, replace at MTBF 6000 hours.
  e) Secondary Cooling Nozzle Blockage: Uneven solidification, internal cracks.
     Inspect and clean nozzles every planned maintenance stop.

3.4 INSPECTION SCHEDULE
  - Per heat: Tundish temperature, mold level control, SEN condition
  - Daily: Mold copper surface inspection, oscillation check, roll alignment
  - Weekly: All zone cooling water flow calibration
  - Per campaign: Full mold change, segment withdrawal and inspection

SECTION 4: HYDRAULIC SYSTEM (Central Hydraulic Power Unit)
System Pressure: 150–300 bar | Oil Volume: 5000 L | Flow: up to 500 L/min
================================================================================

4.1 CRITICAL SENSOR PARAMETERS & ALARM LIMITS
  - System Pressure (bar): Normal 150–280, Low Alarm <150, High Alarm >300
  - Oil Temperature (°C): Normal 30–65, Warning >65, Alarm >70 (viscosity breakdown)
  - Reservoir Oil Level (%): Normal 60–100, Low Alarm <60
  - Filter Differential Pressure (bar): Change filter at ΔP >3.5 bar
  - Flow Rate (L/min): Varies by demand; low flow alarm indicates pump wear
  - Oil Contamination (NAS class): Target NAS 8, Alarm NAS 10+

4.2 COMMON FAILURE MODES
  a) Pump Wear: Reduced flow, increased noise, pressure fluctuations.
     Check pump efficiency quarterly; replace at <85% volumetric efficiency.
  b) Seal Failure: External leaks, pressure loss.
     Inspect all cylinder seals monthly; hydraulic fluid HLP 46 preferred.
  c) Valve Sticking: Proportional valve contamination from dirty oil.
     Oil cleanliness critical — change filters per schedule.
  d) Overheating: High ambient temperature, clogged heat exchanger.
     Check cooler fins monthly; clean with compressed air.

SECTION 5: LADLE FURNACE (Power: 10–40 MW, Electrode diameter: 500mm)
================================================================================

5.1 CRITICAL SENSOR PARAMETERS & ALARM LIMITS
  - Steel Temperature (°C): Normal 1500–1640, Alarm >1650 (ladle refractory risk)
  - Arc Current (kA): Normal 20–48, Trip >50 (electrode risk)
  - Argon Stirring Flow (NL/min): Normal 300–1100, Min 300 (inadequate stirring)
  - Power (MW): Controlled 10–38, Alarm >40
  - Electrode Position: Auto-regulated; alarm if electrode arm hydraulic fault

5.2 COMMON FAILURE MODES
  a) Electrode Breakage: Thermal shock from cold scrap, electrode thread defect.
     Inspect electrode threads every heat; crack detection dye testing monthly.
  b) Electrode Arm Bushing Wear: Current arcing, overheating.
     Inspect copper contact pads monthly; replace at >2 mm wear.
  c) Argon Lance Blockage: Poor stirring, thermal stratification.
     Purge argon lance before each heat; replace per 20 heats.
  d) Refractory Erosion: High steel temperature, aggressive slag composition.
     Laser profiling every 20 heats; critical at <100 mm remaining thickness.

SECTION 6: COMPRESSOR (Atlas Copco ZR 900, Oil-free centrifugal, 900 kW)
================================================================================

6.1 CRITICAL SENSOR PARAMETERS & ALARM LIMITS
  - Discharge Pressure (bar): Normal 6–9.5, Alarm >10
  - Discharge Temperature (°C): Normal 30–90, Alarm >100
  - Vibration (mm/s): Normal 0–4, Warning >4, Alarm >5
  - Motor Current (A): Normal 50–140, Alarm >150
  - Speed (RPM): Normal 2950–3050, Trip if deviation >50 RPM from setpoint
  - Air Filter Differential Pressure: Change at ΔP >50 mbar

6.2 COMMON FAILURE MODES
  a) Surge: Unstable operation, flow reversal. Causes: demand drop, valve malfunction.
     Anti-surge valve must be functional — test weekly.
  b) Bearing Failure: Vibration increase, temperature rise.
     MTBF 20,000 hours; vibration monitoring continuous.
  c) Air Valve Failure: Pressure fluctuation, capacity loss.
     Inspect inlet guide vanes quarterly.
  d) Motor Winding Failure: Insulation degradation from moisture or overload.
     Insulation resistance test annually (>100 MΩ).
