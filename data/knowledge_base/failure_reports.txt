FAILURE REPORT FR-2025-0041
Equipment: Rolling Mill — Stand F3 (Finishing Mill)
Date: 2025-09-15 | Shift: B | Duration: 4.2 hours
Severity: HIGH | Classification: Bearing Failure
Reported By: Eng. Suresh Kumar
================================================================================

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
At 14:32, vibration alarm triggered on Stand F3 drive side bearing at 7.2 mm/s
(alarm set at 7.0 mm/s). Bearing temperature rose from 85°C to 127°C over
45 minutes. Production was halted at 15:15 when vibration reached 7.8 mm/s.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
1. Primary cause: Lubricant contamination by process water ingress through
   worn chock seal (observed during disassembly — seal had swollen and cracked)
2. Contributing factor: Last bearing inspection 3800 hours prior vs. 3000-hour
   PM schedule (overdue by 800 hours due to production pressure)
3. Water contamination caused lubricant emulsification — oil analysis post-event
   showed water content 2800 ppm (limit: 200 ppm) and NAS 12 particle count
4. Accelerated bearing fatigue — spalling observed on inner race

FINDINGS:
- SKF 22230 E/C3 bearing inner race: fatigue spalling over 40% of surface
- Chock seal: worn through, water ingress channel confirmed
- Gear box oil: not contaminated (isolated system)

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN:
1. Emergency roll change and bearing replacement (16:15–18:25)
2. Chock seal replaced on both drive and operator sides
3. Bearing housing cleaned and reflushed with clean oil
4. Emergency oil analysis confirmed clean after refill

PREVENTIVE ACTIONS:
1. PM interval reduced to 2500 hours for chock seal inspection
2. Chock seal upgraded to double-lip design (implemented across all 6 stands)
3. Oil sampling frequency increased to bi-weekly
4. Vibration alarm threshold adjusted to 6.5 mm/s (earlier warning)

COST IMPACT: Production loss ~₹85 lakhs | Repair cost ₹3.2 lakhs

LESSONS LEARNED:
- Seal condition inspection is more critical than bearing inspection
- Water contamination is the primary driver of bearing failure in this application
- Respect PM schedules even during high production periods

FAILURE REPORT FR-2025-0052
Equipment: Blast Furnace — Tuyere Position 14 (West side)
Date: 2025-10-08 | Shift: A | Duration: 1.1 hours (tuyere change)
Severity: CRITICAL | Classification: Tuyere Burnout
Reported By: Shift Manager Rajeev Nair
================================================================================

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
At 04:18 (night shift), rapid blast pressure drop observed from 3.8 bar to 2.1 bar
over 8 minutes. Tuyere cooling water outlet temperature at position 14 spiked to
52°C (normal <35°C). Shift manager initiated tuyere burnout protocol.
Hot metal and slag expelled from tuyere 14 blowpipe joint.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
1. Primary cause: Water ingress into tuyere copper body from cooling water line
   micro-crack (fatigue crack due to thermal cycling — tuyere age: 78 days)
2. Contributing factor: Previous maintenance record showed tuyere 14 had minor
   water leak at cooling port repaired 30 days ago — insufficient repair
3. Contributing factor: Blast temperature running at 1280°C (upper limit)
   increased thermal stress on tuyere copper

FINDINGS:
- Tuyere body: catastrophic failure, copper melted at nose
- Cooling water micro-crack confirmed via dye penetrant testing of similar age tuyeres
- 3 other tuyeres of similar age (>60 days) identified for precautionary replacement

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN:
1. Blast reduced to minimum, tuyere 14 blast valve closed
2. Tuyere change completed in 52 minutes (record time — well-practiced crew)
3. Adjacent tuyeres 13 and 15 inspected — no defects found
4. Tuyeres 7, 18, 22 (all >65 days old) replaced as precaution

PREVENTIVE ACTIONS:
1. Tuyere life limit reduced from 90 days to 70 days
2. Cooling water outlet temperature monitoring alarm tightened to 40°C
3. Any repaired tuyere to be replaced within 21 days of repair
4. Weekly dye penetrant testing on tuyeres >50 days old

COST IMPACT: Production interruption 1.1 hours (~₹35 lakhs) | Safe incident — no injuries

FAILURE REPORT FR-2025-0063
Equipment: Continuous Caster — CCM Strand 2
Date: 2025-11-20 | Shift: C | Duration: 6.5 hours (cleanup and restart)
Severity: CRITICAL | Classification: Breakout
Reported By: CCM Shift Supervisor Anita Bose
================================================================================

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
At 22:45, mold level fluctuation detected (±25mm vs. normal ±10mm). Casting speed
was reduced from 1.8 m/min to 1.2 m/min per automatic control. At 22:51, mold level
suddenly dropped to zero — breakout prediction system alerted. At 22:53, molten steel
spillage confirmed at segment 4 (approximately 8m below mold). Emergency stop activated.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
1. Primary cause: SEN (submerged entry nozzle) partial blockage by alumina deposit
   causing asymmetric flow and turbulent mold level
2. Contributing factor: Steel chemistry showed high Al content (0.048% vs. target 0.030%)
   from Ladle Furnace — increased alumina formation rate
3. Contributing factor: Argon injection to SEN was at lower end of range (2.0 NL/min
   vs. recommended 3–6 NL/min) — insufficient to prevent clogging
4. The combination of high Al, low Ar, and nozzle age (1.9 heats) created rapid clogging
   that shifted from manageable to critical in <6 minutes

FINDINGS:
- SEN post-incident: 60% bore blocked by alumina skull
- Mold copper plates: scratched but within repair limits (0.8mm wear, limit 3mm)
- Strand guide segments 3–5: minor shell adherence cleaned off, no roll damage
- Spillage volume estimated: ~2.5 tonnes of steel solidified in secondary cooling zone

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN:
1. Emergency stop and evacuation — no injuries
2. 6.5 hours to solidify, clean, and restart
3. Mold inspection: plates cleaned, taper re-measured and re-set
4. All segments 1–10 inspected: segment 4 roll bearings replaced (heat damage)

PREVENTIVE ACTIONS:
1. SEN change interval reduced from 2.5 heats to 2.0 heats
2. Argon flow to SEN minimum setpoint increased from 2.0 to 4.0 NL/min
3. Al content target tightened: alarm if >0.040%, casting speed reduction if >0.045%
4. Mold level alarm tightened: ±15mm triggers speed reduction, ±20mm triggers stop

COST IMPACT: Production loss 6.5 hours (~₹2.1 crores) | Repair cost ₹18 lakhs

FAILURE REPORT FR-2025-0071
Equipment: Compressor — Air Station 2 (Atlas Copco ZR 900)
Date: 2025-12-01 | Shift: A | Duration: 3 hours
Severity: HIGH | Classification: Bearing Failure — Drive End
Reported By: Utility Engineer Mohit Sharma
================================================================================

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
Compressor vibration monitoring system (continuous) showed progressive increase
in drive-end bearing vibration from baseline 1.8 mm/s to 4.6 mm/s over 3 weeks.
On 2025-12-01 at 06:22, vibration spike to 5.4 mm/s triggered automatic trip.
Compressor shut down safely. Workshop air pressure fell to 5.5 bar (minimum 6 bar).

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
1. Primary cause: Angular contact bearing (SKF 7318 BECBM) fatigue failure
   — inner race crack propagating to surface spalling
2. Contributing factor: Bearing had accumulated 22,400 hours (manufacturer limit 20,000 hours)
   — change was overdue by ~2,400 hours
3. Contributing factor: Bearing re-lubrication interval not followed
   (should be every 2000 hours; last done at 19,200 hours — 3200 hours overdue)
4. Vibration trend data shows early warning was available at 20 days prior but
   no formal trend review meeting was held in that period

FINDINGS:
- Drive-end bearing: inner race cracked, 3 rolling elements damaged
- Non-drive end bearing: slight discolouration — replaced as precaution
- Motor insulation resistance: 320 MΩ — acceptable
- Coupling: no damage

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN:
1. Both bearings replaced (drive end and non-drive end)
2. Oil changed (compressor oil — Atlas Copco Roto Fluid 8000)
3. Full mechanical inspection: no other damage found
4. Compressor returned to service after 3-hour repair

PREVENTIVE ACTIONS:
1. Bearing life tracking added to CMMS — alert at 18,000 hours (20% before limit)
2. Re-lubrication schedule strictly entered in CMMS: every 1500 hours
3. Weekly vibration trend review meeting established
4. Vibration alarm threshold adjusted: Warning 3.5 mm/s, Trip 5.0 mm/s

COST IMPACT: 3-hour downtime | Compressed air rationing to workshop | ₹4.5 lakhs total
